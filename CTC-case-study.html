    DEMENTIA APP: CASE STUDY

    Demonstrating Outcomes via Key Performance Indicators

    OVERVIEW

    The Care Team Connect™ mobile app was developed by the team at Generation Connect in the Spring of 2017. The app helps home care companies involve families in planning engagement activities for clients with dementia and related cognitive challenges. As the head of product, I led the app launch and a year-long alpha pilot with local Visiting Angels home care franchises.

    From March 2017 to March 2018, Generation Connect partnered with Visiting Angels franchises in Central Pennsylvania. With offices in York, Lancaster, and Hanover, and a team of 300 caregivers serving over 300 families, Visiting Angels is the market leader in providing non-medical home care.

    Throughout the pilot, I worked closely with the leadership team at Visiting Angels to measure key performance indicators including client mood outcomes, caregiver churn, average client length of stay, and client lifetime value. Following the pilot, we analyzed outcomes and developed a series of case studies.


    CASE STUDY

    This case involved a male client who was suffering from multiple conditions, including Parkinson’s and dementia. He was experiencing a range of challenging symptoms including hallucinations, loss of motor skills, and aphasia. The client was verbally and physically aggressive towards caregivers, resulting in an abnormally high rate of employee turnover. The agency had planned on terminating services with the client, but opted to implement the Care Team Connect solution as a last resort effort to improve outcomes.

    After deploying the solution, the client embraced engagement activities involving his favorite music, family photos, and meaningful videos. The agency reported drastic improvements in client mood, caregiver satisfaction, caregiver churn, and family satisfaction. The client continued receiving services through end of life - resulting in an additional $189,000 of revenue with improved gross margins due to enhanced staffing efficiencies.

    This case study was presented during a keynote presentation at the 2019 Visiting Angels National Conference and subsequently disseminated nationwide to business owners across their network. It was instrumental in acquiring $150,000 of investment and the participation of a dozen home care agencies to expand the approach.

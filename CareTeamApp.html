    Generation Connect: App Development

    A digital therapeutic platform for in-home dementia care


    MY ROLE

    As the COO of a lean startup, I was responsible for exploring the market opportunity and securing funding to support product development. Through a combination of grants and partnerships with non-medical home care companies, I secured over $700,000 to support product development.

    I was also responsible for hiring and developing a product team. With an aim of building an in-house dev team, I vetted a series of full-stack developers, eventually partnering with an accomplished product engineer to mentor a junior engineer, UI/UX designer, and myself as a product manager.

    With a product team in place, I led the development and deployment of our MVP solution. I worked closely with key stakeholders including business partners (home care executives, researchers) and end users (people with dementia, family members, in-home caregivers), conducting focus groups and user testing to iterate on the MVP model - consistently enhancing user adoption, retention, and satisfaction.


    PRODUCT

    The Generation Connect platform provides people with dementia, their families, and professional caregivers with timely, relevant, disease-specific vetted support interventions and services via a mobile app for curation of meaningful personalized inputs to support clinical care, and the development of algorithms to prompt evidence-based interventions, provide contextual training, and measure outcomes.




    MILESTONES
    • Spring 2018 - Conducted interviews with key stakeholders and developed a functioning prototype to collect clinical outcomes related to music and social engagement sessions.
    • Fall 2018 - Received seed funding from Verizon Foundation and Consumer technology association to deploy the solution across 30 healthcare organizations.
    • Spring 2019 - Published preliminary clinical outcomes, showing improvements in mood management, in Journal of Medical Internet research.
    • Summer 2019Leveraged clinical and business outcomes to obtain an $150,000 contract with Visiting Angels (one of the largest providers of non-medical home care) to implement the solution with 12 agencies across the US.
    • Summer 2020 - Awarded $450,000 of non-dilutive funding via Small Business Innovation Research grant from the National Institute of Health to conduct user research and feasibility testing.
    • Fall 2021 - Published findings from stakeholder focus groups in the Journal of Medical Internet Research.
    • Spring 2022 - Awarded $450,000 of non-dilutive funding via Small Business Innovation Research grant from the National Institute of Health to conduct user research and feasibility testing.

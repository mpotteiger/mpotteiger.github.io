    CONSULTING

    Collaborating with senior care organizations to bridge the digital divide

    OVERVIEW

    In the early 2010’s, smartphone and tablet adoption was growing exponentially; yet, there was a widening digital divide. As of 2013, over 80% of younger adults (under the age of 34) owned a smartphone, while less than 20% of older adults (over the age of 65) owned a smartphone or tablet computer. Despite the potential of technology to connect people, many older adults were apprehensive about using mobile devices and concerned about technology’s impact on younger generations.


    CONSULTING PROJECTS

    After a personal experience (teaching my widowed grandmother how to use an iPad), I started pursuing consulting projects that were focused on bridging the digital divide. From 2013 to 2016, I worked on a variety of projects, partnering with long-term care facilities, non-profits, and academic institutions.


    ReThink Technology Presentations

    I developed a 60-minute, interactive presentation designed to help long-term care facilities, senior center, libraries, and other organizations introduce older adults to mobile technology. The presentation include a live video phone call with my grandmother.

    • Presented at over 100 locations.
    • Attracted groups of up to 200+ people.
    • Inspired hundreds of seniors to enroll technology courses.


    iPad Deployment & Training Workshops

    Through partnership with senior living communities, I developed a curriculum for iPad training and deployment.

    • Deployed 500+ specially configured iPads across over 100 locations.
    • Developed a visual iPad Training Guide for seniors, and sold thousands of copies.
    • Invited to Cupertino to present to Apple’s healthcare division in 2015 and subsequently involved in a pilot sponsored by Apple.


    Learning Management System Development

    In 2016, I developed content and configured a custom learning management system for a global non-profit. The platform is used by 3,000+ senior care organizations using technology to facilitate person-centered engagement activities.

    • Vetted a variety of learning management platforms and led a platform migration.
    • Created custom content for a series of new courses.
    • Customized platform configuration, branding, and integrations.
